The effect of Coronavirus on the economy

The coronavirus crisis has seen many people lose their jobs and unemployment rates have increased all over the world. Millions of workers have also been put on the government-backed furlough scheme, with around 19% of workers furloughed in the UK (Jones, Palumbo, & Brown, 2020). Global growth is projected at -4.9% (International Monetary Fund, 2020) and this is described as the worst decline since the Great Depression of the 1930s. This is a factor of the unemployment but also from a drop in consumer confidence, as shoppers are staying at home to stop the spread of the virus.

In addition to this, the travel industry, which contributed nearly 3 trillion U.S dollars in 2019 (Lock, 2020), was amongst the hardest industries hit this year. This is a result of airlines cutting flights, customers cancelling business trips and holidays and countries introducing travel bans.

Mental Health

In the UK, people are dealing with the coronavirus pandemic in different ways. More than two-thirds of adults (69%) in the UK said they were very/somewhat worried about the effect that COVID-19 is having on their life now (Office for National Statistics, 2020). The most common impact amongst the groups of people was the lack of freedom. More than 4 in 10 adults (44%) said their well-being was affected (Office for National Statistics, 2020). This number was even higher for the population of people who are over 70 years. Factors contributing to the decline in well-being include:

Factor Percentage
Feeling worried about the future 63%
Feeling stressed or anxious 56%
Feeling bored 49%
